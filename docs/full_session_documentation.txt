================================================================================

PROJECT: "ProteinIK" — a protein-folding-process-inspired IK solver for a UR5
6-DOF robotic arm, benchmarked honestly against five other IK methods, with a
FastAPI backend (live WebSocket solve streaming + batch benchmarking) and a
React + Three.js frontend that renders all solvers live, side by side.

GROUND RULE established by the user (and saved to memory):
  >> Iterate on the `protein_upgrade` branch, NOT on `main`. <<
  All work this session was done on protein_upgrade.

USER'S GOAL (in their words, paraphrased):
  "The initial idea was an IK solver inspired by protein folding. It works and
   beats the base models, but it doesn't win against TRAC-IK. To unlock its
   potential we need to deeply understand protein folding biology first, then
   propose a plan, implement it, run the benchmarks, and iterate until a true
   success is unlocked."

DEFINITION OF "TRUE SUCCESS" adopted:
  ProteinIK must match or beat TRAC-IK on success rate (the primary metric)
  across scenarios, while keeping its self-collision-avoidance edge and staying
  time-competitive (far faster than the other population method, multi-start).

 PART 2 — THE DEEP PROTEIN-FOLDING ANALYSIS
 (full version in docs/protein_folding_deep_dive.md)
================================================================================

THE ONE REFRAME THAT DROVE EVERYTHING
  A protein does NOT fold fast because it runs a clever search. A folding
  molecule does almost the dumbest thing possible: thermal Brownian motion +
  sliding downhill in free energy, with no memory, no global view, no gradient
  computation. It folds in microseconds-milliseconds anyway because the ENERGY
  LANDSCAPE has been shaped — by physics and ~3.5 billion years of evolution —
  so that dumb local physics succeeds. The intelligence is in the LANDSCAPE,
  not the SEARCH.

  Lesson for IK: stop making the *search* mimic folding stages (that is what
  V1/V2 did, as metaphor). Instead (a) shape the objective landscape so trivial
  descent works, and (b) give the solver the things real fast folding has that
  ours lacked: thermal/stochastic acceptance, ensemble parallelism, and a
  barrierless quadratic endgame.

CORE CONCEPTS (each mapped to IK leverage)

  Levinthal's paradox
    10^47+ conformations can't be searched; folding must be GUIDED. ProteinIK's
    Stage-3 random coordinate search is exactly the blind sampling nature
    abandoned — the weakest part, not the signature feature.

  Thermodynamics: free energy G = H - TS, not energy
    Folding minimizes FREE energy; entropy is half the physics and the solver
    ignored it. The hydrophobic effect (the dominant driving force) is ENTROPIC
    (water ordering). Proteins are only marginally stable (5-15 kcal/mol).

  Energy landscape theory — the FUNNEL (deepest section)
    Funnel, not golf course: many high-energy unfolded states at the rim
    narrowing to one native state at the bottom; reaction coordinate Q = fraction
    of native contacts. On average every downhill-in-energy move also increases
    Q — local descent correlates with global progress. That defeats Levinthal.

  Minimal frustration (Bryngelson-Wolynes) — the crown jewel
    Evolution selected sequences whose native interactions are mutually
    consistent (not frustrated/glassy), giving a SMOOTH funnel. Foldability ~
    T_f/T_g ratio. IK analog = problem conditioning + homotopy/continuation +
    a monotone reaction coordinate. This is where ProteinIK's genuine novelty
    claim lives (constructing a smooth low-frustration sub-problem sequence).

  Roughness + thermal escape
    Real funnels have shallow traps; the molecule hops them via kT (Metropolis-
    like, accepts uphill with prob ~ e^(-dE/kT)). A greedy "accept-only-better"
    search cannot escape even a 1-kT bump. => add Metropolis acceptance + cooling.

  Downhill / barrierless folding & the speed limit  (THE SPEED LEVER)
    The fastest proteins fold with NO barrier — pure descent, diffusion-limited.
    The funnel bottom is locally QUADRATIC — exactly where Newton/Gauss-Newton
    dominates. ProteinIK did random +/- steps there (slowest possible). =>
    add an adaptive Levenberg-Marquardt endgame.

  Hierarchy & sequence
    - Nucleation-condensation: a small folding nucleus forms (rate-limiting),
      the rest condenses around it. => find & solve the high-leverage DOFs first.
    - Foldons (Englander): cooperative units added one at a time, each templating
      the next; once formed they lock in and shrink the problem. => progressive
      DOF freezing.
    - Contact order: low CO (local contacts) folds fast/cheap; high CO (long-
      range) is slow/expensive. => order sub-goals cheap-local-first.
    - Co-translational/vectorial folding: N->C, domains fold as synthesized; even
      the SCHEDULE is optimized. => solve base-outward.

  Hydrophobic collapse -> molten globule
    Fast collapse to a compact, native-like-topology but fluid intermediate,
    then slow side-chain annealing: fast topology, slow precision. => pick gross
    configuration/branch first, polish later.

  Chaperones (GroEL/GroES iterative annealing)
    Add NO folding information; fix KINETICS. Encapsulate, isolate from
    aggregation, and ATP-cycle: forcibly partial-unfold a trapped intermediate
    and give it a fresh FAST refold; escalate scope on repeated failure. This is
    exactly ProteinIK's Stage-4 scoped rescue (the part it got most right).

  Statistical-mechanics / ENSEMBLE view  (THE ROBUSTNESS LEVER)
    A test tube folds ~10^15 copies in parallel; "the protein folds" is about a
    DISTRIBUTION collapsing to native. Replica-exchange/parallel-tempering is the
    canonical rough-landscape method. ProteinIK ran a SINGLE chain. The honest
    sting: multi-start beating ProteinIK is the biology telling you parallelism
    is a core folding principle the solver underused.

  UR5-specific gift (not yet exploited)
    The UR5 has a (near) spherical wrist => kinematically DECOUPLABLE (Pieper):
    joints 1-3 = position/reach, 4-6 = orientation. The "foldon hierarchy" is
    mathematically EXACT here and even admits a near-analytical seed — the single
    biggest untapped lever, most likely to attack the latency gap.

RANKED LEVERAGE (mechanism vs metaphor; which gap it attacks)
  1. Barrierless downhill   -> Gauss-Newton/LM endgame        VERY HIGH  (speed)
  2. Ensemble / replica     -> small adaptive parallel set     VERY HIGH  (robustness)
  3. Minimal frustration    -> conditioning + homotopy + Q     HIGH (novel) (both)
  4. Roughness + thermal    -> Metropolis + cooling            HIGH, cheap (robustness)
  5. Foldons                -> progressive DOF freezing         HIGH        (speed)
  6. UR5 wrist decoupling   -> near-analytical seed             HIGH (UR5)  (both)
  7. Nucleation             -> solve high-leverage DOFs first   MEDIUM      (both)
  8. Contact order/vectorial-> schedule DOF entry              MEDIUM      (speed)
  9. Hydrophobic collapse   -> reduce dim / pick branch first   MEDIUM      (robustness)
 10. Iterative annealing    -> partial-unfold + FAST refold     (already in)(robustness)
 11. Local frustration      -> conflict-based "where stuck"     MEDIUM      (robustness)


================================================================================
 PART 3 — DIAGNOSIS: WHY V2 CAPPED AT ~88-96%
================================================================================

The existing V2 (protein_ik.py, solve_protein_ik_v2) already implemented SOME of
the deep-dive ideas: Metropolis acceptance with a cooling schedule, a frustration
index for rescue targeting, contact-order-adaptive stage budgets, and Go-model /
Ramachandran energy terms. Yet it still lost to TRAC-IK. Four concrete reasons,
each mapping to the analysis:

  (1) NO BEST-SO-FAR TRACKING.
      V2 returned its FINAL q. Its own Metropolis acceptance takes uphill moves,
      so it can walk AWAY from a good basin and return a worse config. TRAC-IK
      and multi-start both keep best_q. (Cheap, high-confidence fix.)

  (2) WEAK ENDGAME (no barrierless quadratic descent).
      V2's refinement used a FIXED-damping DLS step (lambda=0.05) with NO
      acceptance test — slow, and can overshoot near the solution. The funnel
      bottom is quadratic; adaptive Levenberg-Marquardt belongs there. (Speed gap.)

  (3) SINGLE TRAJECTORY.
      Real folding is an ensemble; TRAC/multi-start get their ~99% from
      restart/population diversity. V2's scoped rescue is sequential and runs out
      of iteration budget on hard cases. (Robustness gap.)

  (4) POSE-ERROR-ONLY SUCCESS, collision edge unused.
      V2 already had the best collision rate but didn't exploit it when selecting
      among candidates.


================================================================================
 PART 4 — THE PLAN
================================================================================

Build ProteinIK V3 in a NEW file (app/solvers/protein_ik_v3.py), keeping V1 and
V2 intact for comparison, and add the three highest-leverage, biologically
faithful upgrades — measuring each against the benchmark:

  (A) Best-so-far tracking + collision-aware native-state selection.
      Return the lowest-error state VISITED (not the last thermal-kick result);
      among on-target candidates prefer the sterically cleanest ("a clashing
      on-target pose is not the native state — fold again").  [analysis 2.3, 7]

  (B) Adaptive Levenberg-Marquardt endgame ("barrierless downhill").
      Replace fixed-damping DLS. Damping shrinks on each error-reducing step
      (-> Newton-fast in the quadratic basin) and grows on overshoot (-> robust),
      with monotone acceptance.  [analysis 4.4 — the speed lever]

  (C) Adaptive ensemble / replica closer ("the ensemble folds in parallel").
      Run one trajectory; spawn diverse replicas ONLY when it stalls. Easy
      targets stay single-trajectory (fast); hard targets get parallel-search
      robustness.  [analysis 7 — the robustness lever]

The staged-folding identity (target-blind relaxation -> coarse collapse -> funnel
narrowing with Metropolis -> chaperone rescue) is preserved as the per-replica
engine, so V3 keeps ProteinIK's structural, collision-aware character instead of
collapsing into plain multi-start DLS.


================================================================================
 PART 5 — V3 IMPLEMENTATION (app/solvers/protein_ik_v3.py)
================================================================================

STRUCTURE
  _combined_err(spec, q, T_target)
      -> (pos_err, orient_err, combined) where combined = pos + 0.3*orient,
      matching the baselines' best-tracking/acceptance convention.

  _lm_polish(spec, q0, T_target, pos_tol, orient_tol, max_steps, lam0=0.08)
      The (B) adaptive LM endgame. Each step:
        dq = J^T (J J^T + lam^2 I)^-1 err ; q_try = clip(q + dq)
        if combined(q_try) < combined(q): accept; lam = max(lam*0.5, 1e-4)
        else:                              lam = min(lam*2.5, 2.0); break if maxed
      Monotone by construction. Returns (q, combined, converged, steps_used).

  _fold_once(...)
      One folding trajectory with best-so-far tracking (A):
        STAGE 1 (optional, target-blind local relaxation) — coordinate descent on
                neutral-pose + neighbor-smoothness + joint-limit energy.
        STAGE 2 coarse collapse — low-gain DLS pull (hydrophobic collapse analog).
        STAGE 3+4 funnel narrowing — every other iteration, per-joint stochastic
                proposal accepted by Metropolis with an exponential cooling
                schedule (T0=0.3 -> Tf=0.01); each iteration takes a downhill
                step. ONCE INSIDE THE BASIN (pos<0.05, orient<0.2) it hands off
                entirely to _lm_polish (the Newton-fast endgame). On stall
                (stuck_window without progress) it does chaperone rescue with an
                escalation ladder [1,3,5,n] joints, targeting the most frustrated
                joint (frustration_index), resetting the funnel radius.
      Returns (best_q, best_combined, success, iters, rescues).

  solve_protein_ik_v3(...)  — the (C) adaptive ensemble driver:
      1. Contact-order-inspired difficulty scaling of the per-replica budget,
         from reach ratio and Jacobian condition number.
      2. FAST PATH: _lm_polish from q0 (up to 30 steps). Accept immediately ONLY
         if it converges AND is sterically clean (min self-distance >= 0). This
         puts the easy majority near DLS/TRAC speed without surrendering the
         collision win on clash-prone cases.
      3. If not accepted, loop up to max_replicas: replica 0 reuses the seed,
         later replicas use diverse random seeds (Stage-1 skipped — the LM
         endgame does the real convergence). Track the global best.
      4. COLLISION-AWARE NATIVE-STATE SELECTION (A): on convergence, if the pose
         is clean (>=0) stop; if it is CLASHING (penetrating), fold once more for
         a cleaner one (capped at 2 converged candidates total — diminishing
         returns when the target itself forces a strained pose). Among converged
         candidates, return the one with the largest min self-distance.
      5. Stability-checked termination (native kinetic stability): jitter the
         result; if most trials blow up the error, reject as a knife-edge point.

  Tunables landed on: max_iters=150, max_replicas=6, stage1_iters=6,
  stage2_iters=10, stuck_window=10, stuck_eps=2e-4, converged-candidate cap=2.

REGISTRATION
  registry.py: imported solve_protein_ik_v3; added "protein_ik_v3" to
  SOLVER_REGISTRY and "ProteinIK V3" to SOLVER_DISPLAY_NAMES.

================================================================================
 PART 8 — HONESTY CAVEATS (kept in the spirit of this codebase)
================================================================================

  1. LATENCY, NOT SPEED-OF-LIGHT. V3 is ~5x slower than TRAC-IK per query. The
     win is robustness (success) + steric quality (collision), not throughput.
     If single-query latency is the ONLY thing that matters, TRAC-IK is still the
     right choice. For a 6-DOF arm, ~50 ms is comfortably real-time.

  2. BENCHMARK HARNESS HAS RUN-TO-RUN NOISE. run_benchmarks.py seeds each solver
     via hash((seed, trial, name)); Python salts string hashing per process
     (PYTHONHASHSEED), so absolute numbers wobble +/-1-3% between runs. V3's
     ORDERING wins (highest success, lowest collision) held across every run;
     the 600-solve confirmation averages 2 seeds to damp this.

  3. "COLLISION RATE" IN CLUTTERED IS PARTLY TARGET-IMPOSED. Cluttered targets are
     generated near self-collision; some can only be REACHED in a strained pose,
     so no solver can drive that to zero. V3 is lowest because its energy term and
     native-state selection avoid clash WHERE THE TARGET PERMITS it.

  4. THE NOVELTY IS THE STAGED SEQUENCING + THESE THREE LEVERS, not any single
     energy term (each term has precedent in the IK literature). V3's defensible
     claim: a frustration/funnel-aware, collision-selecting, ensemble-folding
     solver that beats production baselines on success AND steric quality.

  5. DECOUPLED-WRIST ANALYTICAL SEEDING IS NOT YET USED. The biggest remaining
     lever (UR5 spherical-wrist Pieper decoupling as the "folding nucleus") is
     left for future work and would most likely attack the latency gap.

 PART 11 — PROTEINIK V4: SPEED OPTIMIZATION PASS (added after V3 shipped)
 (full write-up in docs/v4_optimization.md)
================================================================================

GOAL: bring ProteinIK's mean solve time DOWN while staying inside the protein-
folding domain (no pivot to a different algorithm family; minor pivots allowed
only as optimizations). Kept as a separate solver "protein_ik_v4" / "ProteinIK
V4" so V3 stays intact for comparison.

WHAT DID *NOT* WORK (recorded honestly):
  A first attempt changed the SEARCH itself, framed biologically:
    - gate the stochastic sweep to "stalled" (rough-funnel) iterations,
    - perturb only a 3-joint folding "nucleus" (nucleation-condensation),
    - hand off to the LM endgame earlier (pos < 0.1 instead of 0.05).
  Controlled, warm, same-seed measurement vs V3: SLOWER (~40 ms vs 32 ms) with
  MORE iterations (52-55 vs 42). The nucleus-only search explored less
  effectively (needed more funnel iterations to reach the basin), and the looser
  LM handoff entered the 12-step endgame before the basin was quadratic and
  wasted polish steps. The earlier smoke test that looked 4x faster was an
  artifact: V3 ran first and absorbed cold-start/import warmup, biasing it.
  LESSON: the search was already well-tuned; don't touch it -- profile instead.

WHAT THE PROFILER SHOWED (cProfile over 150 solves):
  - geometric_jacobian DOMINATED (cumulative 3.386s of 8.0s), and most of its
    cost was numpy.cross's per-call overhead (normalize_axis_tuple + moveaxis),
    NOT the arithmetic.
  - Every gradient / LM step computed forward kinematics TWICE: once via
    end_effector_pose() for the pose, again inside geometric_jacobian() for J.
  => The folding LOGIC was never the bottleneck; the linear-algebra PRIMITIVES
     in the hot loop were.

THE ACTUAL OPTIMIZATION (behavior-preserving):
  A single fused primitive, _fast_pose_jac(spec, q), computes the FK chain ONCE
  and derives BOTH the end-effector pose AND the geometric Jacobian from it, with
  the cross products written out explicitly (no np.cross). It is bit-identical to
  geometric_jacobian (verified 0.0 max difference over 2000 random configs), so
  V4's solve TRAJECTORY is identical to V3's -- same iterations, same success,
  same collision rate -- just fewer and cheaper float ops per step. V4 uses this
  fused primitive in the coarse collapse, the funnel descent, the LM endgame
  (_lm_polish_fast), and the fast path. EVERYTHING ELSE IS V3 UNCHANGED (same
  full-chain Metropolis funnel, same chaperone rescue, same adaptive ensemble,
  same collision-aware native-state selection, same stability check).

  Note: core/kinematics.py geometric_jacobian was deliberately left UNCHANGED so
  the baselines are unaffected and the V3-vs-V4 speedup is cleanly attributable
  to V4 alone.

RESULT
  Controlled warm same-seed (150 solves): V3 31.9 ms / 42 iters / 149 ok / 12
  collisions  vs  V4 19.1 ms / 42 iters / 149 ok / 12 collisions  = 1.67x faster,
  identical behavior.

  Noise-averaged benchmark (300 solves/cell, 2 seeds):
    open_space     V3 100.0/ 7.7/43.4   V4  99.7/ 6.3/25.1   speedup 1.73x
    near_singular  V3 100.0/19.3/52.9   V4 100.0/18.3/26.8   speedup 1.97x
    cluttered      V3 100.0/34.0/56.5   V4 100.0/36.3/26.3   speedup 2.15x
    (success% / collision% / mean ms)

  V4 keeps ALL of V3's wins (highest success + lowest collision of all 9 solvers,
  beating both production baselines on both axes) and now solves in a steady

 PART 12 — GENERALITY AUDIT: IS PROTEINIK HARDCODED TO THE UR5?
 (reproducible via backend/test_generalization.py)
================================================================================

QUESTION RAISED: Is this solver overfit to the exact UR5 6-DOF arm, or is it a
general IK solver (more DOF, different robots, other IK applications)?

CODE AUDIT: No hardcoded UR5/6-DOF constants in the solver. Everything is written
against `RobotSpec` and `spec.n_joints`. The only "6" in the hot loop is the
TASK-space dimension of the geometric Jacobian (6 x n = 3 position + 3
orientation), which is correct for ANY degree of freedom -- it is NOT the joint
count. The "6"s that appear (max_replicas=6, stage1_iters=6) are budgets/iteration
counts, not robot dimensions.

PROOF BY EXECUTION (zero code changes; targets = FK(random q) so always reachable
regardless of DOF; success% / mean ms):

  Generality across DOF and kinematics:
    Robot                                 DLS         TRAC-IK     ProteinIK V4
    UR5 (6DOF, the tuned-on robot)        (baseline)  ~99%        100%
    Puma560 (6DOF, different kinematics)  70.0%       98.8%       100.0%
    KUKA iiwa (7DOF, REDUNDANT)           86.2%       100.0%      100.0%
    Synthetic 4DOF                        82.5%       100.0%      100.0%

  Scale robustness (UR5 link lengths scaled; reach 0.12 m .. 12 m):
    Scale         TRAC-IK     ProteinIK V4
    x10 (12 m)    100.0%      100.0%
    x1  (1.2 m)   100.0%      100.0%
    x0.1 (0.12 m)  80.0%      100.0%   <-- V4 MORE robust than TRAC at small scale

CONCLUSION: ProteinIK is a GENERAL serial-revolute-manipulator IK solver. It
generalizes across DOF (4/6/7, including redundant), across kinematics
(UR5/Puma/iiwa/synthetic), and across a 100x size range -- with no code changes.
The UR5 is merely the default robot in the benchmark/frontend HARNESS; the solver
functions take any RobotSpec. The absolute-length thresholds (LM handoff 0.05 m,
collision margin 0.05 m) are ~1 m-scale tuned but did NOT break across 100x scale.

HONEST SCOPE BOUNDARIES (design limits, not overfitting):
  1. Serial REVOLUTE manipulators via standard DH only -- no prismatic joints.
  2. Full 6D pose targets (DLS solves a 6D task, np.eye(6)). Position-only or
     weighted-task IK would need the error vector + eye(6) adapted (a small
     change, not a rewrite).
  3. Serial chains only -- no parallel/closed-chain, mobile bases, or multi-arm.
  4. A few absolute-length thresholds are length-scale assumptions; empirically
     fine across 100x scale, could be made scale-relative for cleanliness.

SUGGESTED FUTURE HARDENING toward "all sorts of IK": (a) scale-relative
thresholds, (b) position-only / weighted-task target mode, (c) prismatic-joint
support in the Jacobian.
